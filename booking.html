Boarding Consent & Vet Alliance

To safeguard all our feline boarders, I require confirmation of up-to-date vaccinations and a signed veterinary release authorizing care in the rare event of emergency clinical treatment.

  • Feline Enteritis and Cat Flu vaccination certificate required
  • Consent for emergency triage transport and diagnostics
  • Details of your current primary veterinary practice

How to Prepare Your Feline

1. Secure Carrier

Safe transportation

Always transport your cat in a secure, hard-shelled carrier with familiar towels inside to block scary parking noises.

2. Diet Portioning

Avoid stomach stress

Pre-pack your cat's dry, raw, or wet meals in individual daily containers to ensure precise diet tracking.

3. Clinic Contacts

Emergency sync

Provide written medical authorization notes stating that Elena is allowed to sync with your veterinary clinic.

Travel Preparation: The Pet Carrier

The journey to the boarding facility is often the most stressful part of travel for a cat. I recommend placing a cozy towel or worn clothing item inside their hard-shelled carrier to provide familiar scents. Spraying the carrier interior with synthetic pheromones 15 minutes before leaving home helps soothe transition anxieties and keeps them calm during the car ride.
