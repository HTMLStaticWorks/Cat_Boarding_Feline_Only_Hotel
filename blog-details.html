How to Reduce Boarding Stress

An expert guide on feline psychology and preparing your cat for transition.

Boarding a cat can be a highly stressful experience if not managed with care. Unlike dogs, cats are strongly tied to their territory. When removed from their home, their sense of safety is compromised. Understanding their instinctual behaviors can help you ease their boarding transition.

1. Scent Comfort is Key

Cats navigate the world through scent. Bringing their favorite blanket or a worn t-shirt belonging to you will provide immediate comfort inside their suite. The familiar smells trigger secure behavior patterns, helping them relax and eat.

2. Vertical Safety Zones

In nature, cats climb high trees to survey territory and avoid predators. In a boarding suite, vertical shelves and ledges are critical. A cat that can sit high up feels safe because they can monitor their environment. This is why I build all suites with custom climbing levels.

3. Boarding Checklist

To ensure a stress-free experience, prepare these items before bringing your cat to my hotel:

  • Their regular dry or wet food (to avoid digestive stress)
  • A familiar blanket or cushion
  • Familiar toys for play sessions
  • Clear medication logs if insulin or pills are required
